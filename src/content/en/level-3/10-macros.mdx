# Instant Automation.

Have you ever had to make the same change on 50 different lines? Instead of going crazy, use **Macros**. Macros allow you to record a sequence of keys and play it back as many times as you want.

### How to Record a Macro
1. Press `q` followed by a letter (e.g., `qa`). Recording starts in register `a`.
2. Perform your actions (e.g., `I` to go to the start of the line, type something, press `<ESC>`).
3. Press `q` again to stop recording.

### How to Play a Macro
- `@a`: Runs the macro recorded in `a`.
- `@@`: Repeats the last executed macro.
- `10@a`: Runs the macro 10 times.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> When recording a macro, always end with a motion that brings the cursor to the correct position for the next line (e.g., `j` or `0`).
